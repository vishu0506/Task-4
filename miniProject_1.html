Welcome to Vaishali (ancient city)

The land of ancient democracy and spirituality. Mahavira, the 24th Tirthankara of Jainism. Born into a royal kshatriya family in what is now Vaishali district of Bihar. He abandoned all worldly possessions at the age of 30 and became an ascetic. He is considered a slightly older contemporary of the Buddha. Chetaka, King and ruler of the Vajjika League which had its capital in Vaishali. Vimalakirti, the central figure of the Vimalakirti Sutra and a lay practitioner of Buddhism. Gayadhara, 11th century Buddhist master and scholar

Vaishali is an important archaeological and historical location in Bihar, India. It is considered the world's first republic and holds religious significance for both Jains and Buddhists.
